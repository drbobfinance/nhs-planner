ONS Public Sector Finances HIGHEST PRIORITY
3rd week of month
Check ONS release for new borrowing and debt figures
Released around the 21st of each month. Look for: PSND (Public Sector Net Debt), PSNB (Public Sector Net Borrowing), and the monthly deficit figure. These are the headline numbers for your site.
→ ONS Public Sector Finances page
Update debt-clock.html with new PSND figure
The live debt clock uses the PSND figure as its base. Update the starting value and the date it was recorded. Ask Claude to do this — paste in the new ONS figures and say "update the debt clock."
Update index.html stats strip with new figures
The homepage shows headline debt, borrowing and per-person figures. Update these to match the new ONS release. Ask Claude: "update the homepage stats with the latest ONS figures: [paste figures here]"
Post on X about the new figures
This is timely content — post it the day the ONS releases the data. Use this template:
📱 X Post Template — replace [FIGURES] with actual numbers
ONS just released the latest UK borrowing figures. Debt: £[NEW DEBT FIGURE]bn — [UP/DOWN] from last month. Monthly deficit: £[MONTHLY FIGURE]bn. Year-to-date borrowing: £[YTD FIGURE]bn — [above/below] OBR forecast. Full picture 👇 uk-debt.info/debt-clock.html

Bank of England MPC Decision HIGH
Every 6 weeks
Check Bank Rate decision
The Monetary Policy Committee meets every 6 weeks. Check bankofengland.co.uk for the decision. If rates change, this affects your QE/QT cost calculations and future interest scenarios.
→ Bank of England Bank Rate page
If rate changes: update understanding-qe-qt.html and future-interest.html
The QE/QT page shows the annual cost at current Bank Rate. If the rate changes, ask Claude to update the figures. The future interest page shows scenarios — check the current rate is correctly shown.
Post on X if rates change
Rate decisions get a lot of engagement on social media. Post immediately after the 12 noon announcement.
📱 X Post Template — rate cut version
Bank of England just cut rates to [NEW RATE]%. What this means for UK debt interest costs: At [NEW RATE]%: costs £[FIGURE]bn/year At previous [OLD RATE]%: was £[FIGURE]bn/year Every cut saves real money on the QE bill. uk-debt.info/understanding-qe-qt.html

ONS Labour Market Data MEDIUM
2nd or 3rd Tuesday of month
Check unemployment rate and employment figures
Released monthly. Look for: unemployment rate, employment rate, and real wage growth. If unemployment moves significantly (above 5.5% or below 4.5%), update your employment.html page.
→ ONS Labour Market Statistics
Check if US jobs revision is significant this month
Released first Friday of each month (US time — early afternoon UK). Check if the revision to previous months is large (over 50,000). If so, it's worth posting about on X — your jobs data accuracy page is very relevant.
→ BLS Employment Situation release

OBR / Budget Events HIGH when they happen
March + Autumn
Autumn Budget 2026 — update obr-spring-2026.html with new forecasts
The next major fiscal event is the Autumn Budget, likely October or November 2026. When it happens, ask Claude to update the OBR forecast table and all the key figures. This is your biggest content opportunity of the year.
→ OBR website
When Budget happens: post a thread on X and a long LinkedIn post
The Budget is your highest-traffic moment of the year. Post immediately after the Chancellor sits down. Ask Claude to draft your X thread and LinkedIn post based on the new OBR figures — takes 5 minutes.

Annual Data Releases MEDIUM
Various months
UBS Global Wealth Report (July each year) — update uk-wealth-decline.html
Published annually in July. This is the source for your wealth decline page. When the new report comes out, ask Claude to update the figures and the country comparison chart.
→ UBS Global Wealth Report
BLS Annual Benchmark Revision (February each year) — update jobs-data-accuracy.html
The US Bureau of Labor Statistics publishes its annual benchmark revision every February. This is when major job count revisions are announced. Always worth a social media post — it's your most-shared content type.
→ BLS Benchmark Revision page
OBR Fiscal Risks Report (every 2 years) — update fiscal-trajectory.html
The OBR publishes a Fiscal Risks and Sustainability report every two years. This contains the long-term debt projections (the 275% by 2070s figure). When it updates, check if the long-term forecast has changed.
