🚨 40% of UK adults could not cover one month's essential expenses if they lost their income. 4.1 million adults are in problem debt. Insolvencies reached 126,240 in 2025 — the highest since 2010. The combination of elevated interest rates, high rents and squeezed real wages has left a significant minority of UK households financially exposed.

📋 The UK's total debt across all sectors — government, households and corporates — is approximately £6.6 trillion, around 222% of GDP. This is one of the highest ratios in the developed world, reflecting decades of credit expansion, rising house prices and government borrowing. The household and corporate debt is mostly not a fiscal problem (it is owed between private parties) but it does create systemic financial stability risk.
