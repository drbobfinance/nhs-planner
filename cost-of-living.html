🚨 The annual rate of inflation peaked at 11.1% in October 2022 — a 41-year high — before easing over the subsequent 18 months. During the worst period, 91% of adults in Great Britain reported a higher cost of living than the previous month. Real wages fell for two consecutive years — the sharpest drop in recorded UK history.

Ofgem Energy Price Cap — Annual Household Bill 2019–2026
⚠️ The energy price cap rose from £1,042 in 2020 to a peak of £3,549 in Q1 2023. The Government's Energy Price Guarantee capped bills at £2,500 for most of 2023, costing £37bn in taxpayer subsidies. Bills have since fallen but remain ~20% above pre-crisis levels. Over 6.5 million households are in fuel poverty.

🚨 The legacy of the crisis: Even as inflation has fallen back, the damage is permanent — prices don't reverse. A household basket that cost £400/month in 2020 now costs ~£520. The extra £120/month compounds year after year. For the bottom 40% of earners, who spend a much higher share of income on essentials, the real living standard loss is severe and ongoing.
