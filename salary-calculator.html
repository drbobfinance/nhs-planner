📋 The UK salary distribution is strongly right-skewed — most workers earn between £20,000 and £50,000, but a small number of very high earners stretch the top of the distribution. ⚠️ The striking fact: The top 10% of earners pay 60.2% of all income tax. The top 1% alone pay 28.2%. Yet the top decile pays roughly the same effective total tax rate (~45–55%) as the bottom decile when all taxes (particularly VAT, fuel duty and council tax) are included — because indirect taxes are regressive, falling proportionally harder on lower incomes.
🎯 Pension Savings Planner — How Much Should You Save?
The UK government's Pension Advisory Service suggests a pension pot of approximately £250,000 is needed for a modest retirement income (combined with state pension). However, inflation erodes the real value of money — £250,000 today will be worth significantly more in nominal terms by the time you retire. ⚠️ Inflation warning: At 4% annual inflation, £250,000 today is equivalent to approximately £456,000 in 33 years. Your pension target must be set in FUTURE money, not today's money, to maintain the same purchasing power. The chart and calculations adjust for this automatically.
✅ The power of starting early: At 6% annual return, a 25-year-old needs to save ~£250/month to reach a £250,000 (real terms) pot by 68. A 45-year-old needs ~£900/month for the same outcome. Starting 20 years earlier requires nearly 4× less per month. Tax relief on pension contributions (20% basic rate, 40% higher rate) means the government tops up every £1 you put in — it is the most tax-efficient saving available.
