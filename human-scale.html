The Concept — Making the Unimaginable Relatable

It is impossible to intuitively grasp numbers like "£2.91 trillion of debt." So this page converts the entire UK national finances into the equivalent for a single working person earning the UK median salary of £39,039 per year. Every national figure is divided by GDP (£2,968bn) and multiplied by £39,039 to give a proportional personal equivalent.

📐 The formula: Human equivalent = (National figure ÷ UK GDP) × Median annual salary. For example, national debt of £2.92tn ÷ GDP of £2.97tn = 98% of GDP. 98% × £39,039 salary = £38,258 personal debt equivalent.

🚨 The sobering conclusion: If the UK government were a single person earning £39,039 a year, they would owe £66,025 in total liabilities — nearly 1.7× their annual salary. They would be spending £4,602 more per year than they earn, adding to their debt every single day. And their "hidden" pension promise amounts to another £18,412 on top. This is the fiscal reality of modern Britain, made relatable.
